Rainbow

Colorizes enclosed content with one continuous rainbow char-by-char, where client-rendered content like the Keybind-Tag and its companions cannot, due to inherent limitations, be split up and will just be considered as an atomic unit.

AttributeTypeMandatoryDefaultDescription
override-colorsScalarnofalseWhether to override already colorized members
skip-whitespaceScalarnotrueWhether to skip whitespace when coloring
skip-non-textScalarnofalseWhether to skip atomic units like <key/>
merge-innerScalarnofalseWhether to merge inner rainbows for continuity
deepScalarnofalseWhether to dive into children's markup-attributes
phaseScalarno0.0Phase-offset of the transition; 0-100%

Examples​

Continuous Rainbow​

The simplest form of a rainbow, using all standard attribute-values.

<rainbow>All of the fancy rainbow colors!</>

Injected Color​

By default, colorized members will keep their style and the transition will just skip over them.

<rainbow>All of the <red>fancy</> rainbow colors!</>

Split Individual Rainbows​

In the following example, the text 'hellohello-worldworld-testtest' is to be colorized with individual rainbows per word, having the hyphens shaded in a mere grey-tone.

<rainbow
  *for-word="'hellohello-worldworld-testtest' @ '-'"
  for-separator={<gray>-}
>{{word}}</>

The above could also be specified without making use of advanced features like loops and the explode-operator, just like the following example portrays:

<rainbow>hellohello</><gray>-</><rainbow>worldworld</><gray>-</><rainbow>testtest</>

But the prior style allows for great flexibility when it comes to data-driven rendering, as injected monochrome passages may now be dynamically defined by the environment.

Split Continuous Rainbow​

When the merge-attribute is enabled, inner rainbows will be joined in order to allow for visual continuity.

<rainbow merge-inner=true>
  <rainbow
    *for-word="'hellohello-worldworld-testtest' @ '-'"
    for-separator={<gray>-}
  >{{word}}</>
</>

Animated Split Continuous Rainbow​

By binding the phase-offset to an environment-variable, all while altering said variable in-between calls to render, animations may be created.

<rainbow
  merge-inner=true
  [phase]="current_phase"
>
  <rainbow
    *for-word="'hellohello-worldworld-testtest' @ '-'"
    for-separator={<gray>-}
  >{{word}}</>
</>
